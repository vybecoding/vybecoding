Module 1: Introduction to AI Agents

15 min read • Lesson 1 of 3

What are AI Agents?

AI agents are autonomous software systems that can perceive their environment, make decisions, and take actions to achieve specific goals. Unlike traditional chatbots that simply respond to queries, AI agents can actively plan, execute tasks, and learn from their interactions.

Key Characteristics

  • Autonomy: Agents can operate without constant human intervention
  • Reactivity: They respond to changes in their environment
  • Proactivity: They can take initiative to achieve goals
  • Social ability: They can interact with other agents or humans

Why Claude for AI Agents?

Claude's advanced language understanding and generation capabilities make it an excellent foundation for building sophisticated AI agents. With its ability to understand context, follow complex instructions, and generate human-like responses, Claude can power agents that feel natural and intelligent.

Example: Simple Claude Agent

import anthropic

class SimpleAgent:
    def __init__(self, api_key):
        self.client = anthropic.Anthropic(api_key=api_key)
        
    def process_task(self, task):
        response = self.client.messages.create(
            model="claude-3-opus-20240229",
            max_tokens=1024,
            messages=[{
                "role": "user",
                "content": f"As an AI agent, help me with: {task}"
            }]
        )
        return response.content
